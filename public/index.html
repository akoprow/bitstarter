    How does it work?

    1 Go for your training, while wearing your device of choice to collect workout data (see the following point).
    2 Upload your workout data to Fitspector. It will accept TCX and GPX industry standard file formats. That means you can use most Garmin and Polar watches, as well as GPS-enabled smartphones (both Android and iPhone).
    3 Upload your workout data to Fitspector and enjoy beautiful visualizations that will help you reveal trends and better understand your workouts. After launch and for the first year Fitspector will provide one new visualization every month, so there will always be something new to try and look forward to.
    Heads up: a demo of a sample visualization coming up soon; stay tuned!

    FAQ

    What happens when the funding period is over?
    Two options. If Fitspector reaches it's funding target of $15,000, giving us means to bring it to life then you can sit down, relax and wait for it to come life! (also see the following question).
    When will Fitspector be ready to use?
    The precise launch date is yet to be determined but we seriously hope to deliver you a nice Christmas present this year!
    How will I be able to follow progress of Fitspector?
    Just follow the Fitspector's blog; on average expect a post every two weeks or so. I'll also post updates (and links to posts) on Twitter and Facebook.
    Who can benefit from Fitspector?
    In principle anyone doing fitness who is interested in gaining insights into one's training. However, most charts will work best for running and cycling activities.
    What will I need to use Fitspector?
    Please note that Fitspector is not a data collection service. It will allow easy upload of workout data in TCX and GPX file formats, so for starters you'll need a compatible device.
    Depending on demand we will also try to provide easier ways of synchronizing your workout data such as:
    • Dropbox or Google Drive synchronization, that will allow you just to place your workouts files in appropriate folders and have them processed automatically by Fitspector.
    • Automatic synchronization with the RunKeeper account. RunKeeper seems to be the only workout tracking site that provides API access to user's data.
    Keep an eye on the blog for more details.
    What if I have some other question that is not covered here?
    Just send an email to info@fitspector.com. We'll do our best to answer your question within 48 hours.
