    How does it work?

    1 Go for your training, while wearing your device of choice to collect workout data (see the following point).
    2 Upload your workout data to Fitspector. It will accept TCX and GPX industry standard file formats. That means you can use most Garmin and Polar watches, as well as GPS-enabled smartphones (both Android and iPhone).
    3 Upload your workout data to Fitspector and enjoy beautiful visualizations that will help you reveal trends and better understand your workouts. After launch and for the first year Fitspector will provide one new visualization every month, so there will always be something new to try and look forward to.
    Heads up: a demo of a sample visualization coming up soon; stay tuned!

    FAQ

    When will Fitspector be ready to use?
    The precise launch date is yet to be determined but it should be some time in the 3 months window between Nov 2013 and Feb 2014. See also the following question.
    How will I be able to follow progress of Fitspector?
    Just follow the Fitspector's blog; on average expect a post every two weeks or so. I'll also post updates (and links to posts) on Twitter and Facebook.
    How will the pledge money be used?
    The money will be used to pay for:
    • hosting,
    • designer fees (logo, web design, ...),
    • marketing costs.
    It will not be used as my salary, as I pledge to do this project pro bono.
    Who can benefit from Fitspector?
    In principle anyone doing fitness who is interested in gaining insights into one's training. However, most charts will work best for running and cycling activities.
    What will I need to use Fitspector?
    Please note that Fitspector is not a data collection service. It will work by connecting with your RunKeeper account and getting data from there, so you will need a RunKeeper account to use Fitspector. Creating one is easy and absolutely free.

    Why RunKeeper? Not only is it a great service but it also provides an open API allowing external services, such as Fitspector, to leverage its data and provide useful services.

    I'd love to also support Strava users -- another great service -- but unfortunately Strava decided to limit access to their API. I'll try my best to obtain early access and make Fitspector work with Strava. If that fails it will only happen after their APIs are open to the public again.

    I know what it takes to live and breathe sports. I know what data is important for athletes and why. And finally I know how to forge those requirements into a product.
